Koality Health

A healthcare provider search platform designed to help users find providers who match thei medical and personal preferences.

Role: UX Designer and Researcher.

Team: Jordan Lesaca, Jackline Munene, Nihal Sandadi

UX Design Process

Throughout this project, our team followed a human-centered and iterative UX design process. We created low-fidelity sketches for layout ideas before we did the medium fidelity prototypes.

From there, we conducted user research through interviews. We asked people what they look for when finding a provider, what makes the process frustrating, and what would make it easier. Our team then organized those findings using affinity diagrams to pull out recurring themes. We found that users like being able to search and apply filters to find someone that fits their criteria, with preferences on gender, location, and being able to see reviews Then we used task flow diagrams to map out the steps behind the most important user goals.

After identifying key themes, we used task flow diagrams to break down important user goals into smaller steps. This helped us think about how users would move through the system and what screens and interactions were needed to support those tasks.

We then moved into prototyping. Design principles like learnability, consistency, and feedback shaped our decisions throughout designing. The process was iterative. We got feedback through critiques and testing, caught problems early, and revised as we went to improve the design.

We also thought about error prevention and efficiency by making important actions clear, reducing confusion, and keeping common tasks easy to complete.

Overall, our process followed the UX lifecycle we learned during the duration of the project: research, analysis, requirements, ideation, prototyping, evaluation, and revision. Our decisions were based on user needs rather than assumptions.

Interface Description

Our interface is a healthcare provider search platform built for people who want a provider that fits both their medical needs and personal preferences. A lot of existing healthcare platforms can be difficult to navigate when you're looking for someone who has experience with a specific community or shares certain values.

The interface follows this path: landing on the platform, searching for a provider, reviewing results, viewing a profile, and moving toward booking care. Things like convenience, provider gender, and the patient populations a provider serves came up a lot in our research, so we made sure those were easy to find in the design.

Design Evolution

Our low-fidelity wireframes were mostly about structure — where things go, how navigation would work, and what the main actions on each screen should be.

In the medium-fidelity versions we started cleaning up the layout and making decisions about where information should be. By the high-fidelity stage, we had a clearer visual identity and a more complete experience. One bigger shift was the provider profile page where we originally used more profile picture but it started feeling like social media rather than a healthcare platform, so we scappred that idea.

Key Feedback and Changes

Our design decisions were shaped by both research and evaluation. We chose teal as the primary color because it felt calm, professional, and appropriate for healthcare. We paired teal with orange for calls to action so important buttons would stand out more clearly and on the color wheel, these two colors complement each other.

Early expert evaluation identified several issues that influenced later iterations. Feedback pointed out difficulty completing some tasks, too much unused space on certain screens, a weak call to action on the landing page, and a need for a confirmation page after scheduling an appointment. Because of that, we changed the landing page, made important actions more visible, improved our layout and added a clearer scheduling flow.

High-Fidelity Prototype Tasks

The high-fidelity prototype covers the main tasks from our usage scenarios. Users can begin a provider search, review matching providers, view detailed provider profiles, schedule an appointment, confirm the appointment, manage appointments, and leave a review.

Conclusion

This project gave us a clearer picture of what it actually takes to design for something as personal as healthcare. One of the bigger limitations is that right now, Koality Health has no real connection to medical organizations, insurance networks, or provider databases. For this to work as a real product, those partnerships would need to exist so that the information users see is actually accurate and up to date.

Future work could explore how to handle that data integration, as well as how to make the platform accessible across more devices and for users with different abilities.
